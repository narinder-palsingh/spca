The State Police Complaint Authority (SPCA), Haryana was constituted on 16.08.2010 by the State Government under the Haryana Police Act, 2007. Smt. NAVRAJ SANDHU, IAS (Retd.), 1st menber Sh. K.K. MISHRA, IPS (Retd.) & 2nd member Sh. R.C. VERMA, IAS (Retd.) have been appointed as State Police Complaint Authority (SPCA) Haryana. As per section 65 of the Haryana Police Act.

The authority shall enquire into the allegations of “Serious Misconduct” against Police Personnel as detailed below: -

1.) Either Suo-Moto are on a complaint received from any of the following: -
 (i) Victim or any person on his behalf on a sworn affidavit.
 (ii) The National or State Human Rights Commission.

Explanation: - “Serious Misconduct” for the purpose of this Chapter shall mean any Act or omission of a Police Official/Officer, that leads to or amounts to–

 (1) Death in police custody
 (2) Rape or attempt to commit rape
 (3) Grievous hurt in police custody

provided that the authority shall enquire into a complaint or such arrests or detention, only if he is satisfied prima-facie about the veracity of the complaint. Provided further that no anonymous, synonymous and pseudonymous complaints shall be entertained.

2) The Authority may also enquire into any other case referred to it by the Director General of Police or the State Government.

The Authority while conducting inquiry of the cases shall have all the powers of a Civil Court trying a suit under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (Act 5 of 1908) in particular in respect of the following matters: -
    (a) summoning and enforcing the attendance of witnesses and examining them on oath;
    (b) discover and production of any document;
    (c) receiving evidence on affidavits;
    (d) requisitioning any public record or copy thereof from any court or office;
    (e) issuing authorities for the examination of witnesses or documents; and
    (f) any other matter as may be prescribed.

The Authority after completion of the inquiry will communicate his findings to the State Government and the Government shall consider the findings and recommendations of the Authority and take appropriate action.
