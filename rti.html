Information under RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT 2005 (Section 4 (1) b)


1. Particulars of Authority: -


(a) Aims and objectives: - Aims and objectives of the Authority is to inquire into the complaints of ‘serious misconduct’ against police officials/officers. The authority is a Civil Oversight body to provide remedy to people vis-à-vis police and to fix their accountability. This authority ensures that there is no manipulation in the internal departmental inquiry.
(b) Brief history and background for its establishment: - A writ petition under article 32 was filed in the Hon’ble Supreme Court by Sh. Parkash Singh Ex Director General of Police of State of Assam and Uttar Pardesh. In this case (Parkash Singh v/s Union of India 2006) Hon’ble Supreme Court has given six directives to the states. One of them was to establish State Police Complaint Authority. The Haryana Police Act 2007 contains provision for State Police Complaint Authority under Chapter VIII entitled as Police Accountability (Section 59-68) which deals with State Police Complaint Authority in detail.
Sh. Ram Niwas, IAS (Retd.) was appointed State Police Complaint Authority (SPCA) Haryana, on 28.02.2019.
(c) Structure of the Department: - The office of State Police Complaint Authority is located in Old PWD Building, Sector 19-B, Chandigarh.

2. Powers and Duties of the officers and employees:-


State Police Complaint Authority has been given the powers of a civil court trying a suit under section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (Act 5 of 1908) and in particular in respect of the following matters:-
  • • Summoning and enforcing the attendance of witnesses and examining them on oath;
  • • Discover and production of any document;
  • • Receiving evidence on affidavits;
  • • Requisitioning any public record or copy thereof from any court or office;
  • • Iissuing authorities for the examination of witness or documents;
  • • Any other matter as may be prescribed.

3. Procedure for decision making: -


After receiving the complaint Authority proceeds to find out whether prime facie case is made out of serious misconduct or not. After that Authority seeks reports from concerned Commissioner of Police & District Superintendent of Police regarding the matter. If there is need to proceed further State Police Complaint Authority summons both the parties for the purpose of inquiry along with relevant documents if required. After conducting the inquiry State Police Complaint Authority (SPCA) sends the report to the Government recommending action against delinquent officials/officers.

4. Norms for discharge of functions: -


The functions are discharged as per statutory provisions of Haryana Police Act 2007 and the rules and procedure drafted by State Police Complaint Authority (SPCA) which are in consonance with the principles of natural justice and fair play.
